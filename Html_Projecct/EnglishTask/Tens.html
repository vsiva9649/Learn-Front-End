Tense Positive Negative Interrogative Negative Interrogative
Simple Present Tense You look beautiful. You do not look beautiful. Do you look beautiful? Don’t you look beautiful?
Present Continuous Tense I am reading a newspaper. I am not reading a newspaper. Am I reading a newspaper? Am I not reading a newspaper?
Present Perfect Tense I have finished my work. I have not finished my work. Have I finished my work? Haven’t I finished my work?
Present Perfect Continuous Tense I have been working on this project for a week. I have not been working on this project for a week. Have I been working on this project for a week? Haven’t I been working on this project for a week?
Simple Past Tense You spoke really well. You did not speak really well. Did you speak really well? Didn’t you speak really well?
Past Continuous Tense I was listening to the news. I was not listening to the news. Was I listening to the news? Wasn’t I listening to the news? / Was I not listening to the news?
Past Perfect Tense I had completed my assignment before everyone else even started. I had not completed my assignment. Had I completed my assignment before everyone else even started? Hadn’t I completed my assignment before everyone else even started?
Past Perfect Continuous Tense I had been cooking my mom’s favourite dish when she walked into the kitchen. I had not been cooking my mom’s favourite dish when she walked into the kitchen. Had I been cooking my mom’s favourite dish when she walked into the kitchen? Hadn’t I been cooking my mom’s favourite dish when she walked into the kitchen?
Simple Future Tense I will read the newspaper from tomorrow. I will not read the newspaper from tomorrow. Will I read the newspaper from tomorrow? Will I not read the newspaper from tomorrow?
Future Continuous Tense Balu will be performing at the International event in Australia. Balu will not be performing at the International event in Australia. Will Balu be performing at the International event in Australia? Will Balu not be performing at the International event in Australia?
Future Perfect Tense I will have dressed up by the time you reach home. I will not have dressed up by the time you reach home. Will I have dressed up by the time you reach home? Will I not have dressed up by the time you reach home?
Future Perfect Continuous Tense In January, I will have been living in San Francisco for ten years. In January, I will not have been living in San Francisco for ten years. In January, will I have been living in San Francisco for ten years? In January, will I not have been living in San Francisco for ten years?
